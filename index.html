Computational Field Research

A virtual seminar: Fall 2015-Spring 2016

About the Seminar

The goals of the seminar are:

  • To train PhD students in rigorous computational-qualitative research design, methods, and attendant styles of theorizing
  • To initiate a number of computational-qualitative research projects based on PhD dissertations
  • To increase exposure of PhD students to American A-level conferences and journals

Structure

Three main components:

  • Seminar on computational-qualitative research
  • Computational methods training based on online resources
  • Tutoring on computational-qualitative research projects

Data Science Tools

These tools & resources are essential for all participants:

  • Tools
    • R environment for statistical computing. Goes well with RStudio - open source IDE.
    • Github account is used for version control and collaboration. See comprehensive tutorials here and here.
    • Stackoverflow is a Q&A platform with a vibrant community and a lot of answr to typical problems.
  • MOOCs on R and Data Analysis
    • A free Data Science track on Coursera. First three courses give a good overview of R, Rstudio and Github and are suitable for absolute beginners.
    • Various Data Science courses on Udacity. Some courses require subscription.
    • DataCamp courses on R and statistics. Full access requires subscription.
  • Books
    • Advanced R by Hadley Wickham, online.
    • An Introduction to Statistical Learning by James et al., pdf and expert videos.
    • The R Inferno by Patrick Burns, pdf.
  • SQL
    • SQL Zoo in-browser tutorials and practice for SQL.
    • GalaZQL in-broweser interactive SQL tutorial.
    • SQL from Stanford is a self-paced online mini course that covers the basics with more courses here.
  • Helpful resources online
    • R-bloggers one of the most visited resources about R and news from the industry..
    • DataTau - data science online community.
    • Code Academy HTML & CSS free in-browser course.
  • Podcasts
    • The R-podcast
    • Data science podcasts list
  • Twitter
    • @RLangTip offers daily R tips.
    • @R_Programming tweets R learning tips from rstatistics.net
